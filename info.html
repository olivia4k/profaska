Justyna Profaska

Justyna Profaska is an architect and a PhD Fellow in Landscape and Environment at La Sapienza University of Rome. Her research focuses on the perception of space in digital worlds. Through the investigation of landscape and its representation, her goal is to understand the impact of the internet, new media, and virtual reality on humans, as well as new narratives on the landscape. She graduated in 2020 from Poznan University of Technology and studied architecture at the Karlsruhe Institute of Technology and Sapienza University of Rome. During her studies, she worked in the architecture and construction fields in Berlin. She gained international experience working in architecture and design research in Germany, Poland, and Italy. In 2021, she worked as an architect at Studio Fuksas in Rome. During the academic year 2021/2022, she collaborated on the didactics at the Laboratory of Architectural Design III with Prof. Alfonso Giancotti. In 2023, she will assist Prof. Benedetta Di Donato with the course 'Theories of Contemporary Landscape'. Currently, she is a fellow of Ph.D. research in Landscape and Environment in the Architecture Department at Sapienza University of Rome, where she continues her thesis on virtual landscapes.
